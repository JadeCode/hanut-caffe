Methods of Making Coffee

  • "Black Coffee"

    Making Black, or Turkish or: It means you take the finest ground coffee and pour boiling water over it for a few minutes. The coffee will extract most of the caffeine and will be less consentrated than an espresso, but still have a strong taste.

  • "Makinetta"

    Making coffee in a makinetta is a whole ritual, I love it. There are two kinds of makinettas - Mocha and Brika. You could also choose to have a makinetta made out of aluminum or nirosta, each has its own advanteges concurning taste and texture. All makinettas have the same parts:

    • A water container with a safety vale, the water should reach just below it.
    • A basket where the ground coffee goes - you never press down on the coffee or it would get stuck and the makinetta will burn.
    • A container to which the drink itself flows through a thin tube with snall openings at the top.
    The Makinetta Brika has an additional weight at the top of the tube, making a smaller gap and builds more prressure for the coffe to go through and the result is closer to an esspresso.

  • "Esspreso"

    The espresso is a drink more concentrated than the makinneta one, it goes through even tighter openings in the portafilter and in higer temperature. The result is a drink with a rich Crema and aroma that fills the room.

  • "Capuchino"

    A capuchino is a drink made of thirds:

    • A First, an espresso. I would recommend something not too mellow so it would not dissapear.
    • Then, a third of steamed milk, to balance the espresso.
    • And finally, a third or a bit less of foamed milk to top it all
    The word capuchino comes from the Italian word "cappuccio", meaning hood or cap. The foamed milk is the the drink's cappuccio.

  • "Filters"

    Making a filter coffee is making a cup of coffee which is more mild than the espresso, and yet it has different flavours. The way the water goes through the coffee only once leaves not so harsh drink and no coffee in the cup. There are a few kinds of filters: paper, cloth, plasic and metal. Each requires a different width of grounded coffee. I would recommend the stroger-tasting and more acidic kinds of coffees and blends for the filter, so they would become milder and keep thier rich flavour.
    Another kind of filter is the French Press. The french press has a glass cilinder and a thin round metal filter with a handle going up and down. The metal filter pushes the ground cofffee trough the hot water.
